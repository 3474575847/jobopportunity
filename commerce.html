1-Budget Analyst

Duty-A budget analyst is a finance professional who helps a business organise and allocate financial resources by allocating an adequate budget. They create a business's budget report and oversee the spending habits of a business. They evaluate budget proposals, redistribute extra funds and recommend spending cuts. Using cost-benefit analysis, these professionals explore alternative funding methods and review financial requests their organisation receives. A budget analyst communicates their recommendations to executives within an organisation.

2- Financial Controller

Duty- A financial controller is a senior management professional who looks after an organisation's accounting and finance department. These professionals evaluate and managing financial risks and coordinate the audit process. They integrate finance operations like budgeting and forecasting, handle tax matters and ensure financial stability. Also, complying with the state and central regulations related to tax filing and financial reporting is an essential duty of a financial controller.

4-Investment manager

12- Accounts receivable manager

Average salary- ₹37,536 per month

Duty-Accounts receivable managers collect payments from clients on behalf of a company. They primarily process invoices, work with missed payments and manage a team of accounts receivable clerks. 14- Payroll supervisor

Average salary- ₹6,43,359 per year

Duty-The payroll supervisor is responsible for coordinating and reviewing payroll processing operations. They ensure accurate calculations of salary and tax and the company's deductions. A part of their work-day involves preparing statistical reports on commission, bonuses, employee pay, leaves and taxes. To ensure an organisation meets the compliance procedures, a payroll supervisor may recommend and implement tax and payroll processes changes. Employers expect a payroll supervisor to have up-to-date information on the latest government regulation related to taxes and deductions.

15- Credit manager

Average salary- ₹4,65,020 per year

Duty- Credit managers are finance professionals who oversee the credit granting procedure for an organisation. Their primary responsibility is to reduce losses because of bad debt, protect a company's assets, and optimise the sales process by maintaining a credit policy. Apart from evaluating a customer's creditworthiness, these professionals approve and reject loan requests from customers. They negotiate loan terms, calculate and set the loan interest. Also, these professionals review and update the credit policy as per changing requirements and government regulations. Many credit managers may work to create a credit scoring model to reduce the risk of bad debts.
